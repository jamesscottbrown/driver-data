Driving license statistics A series of visualizations of GB Driving Licence Data.

How old are driving licence holders?

There is a sudden surge in provisional driving license registrations at 17, which gradually declines as people switch to full licenses. Provisional licenses for some vehicle types (such as mopeds) can be obtained by people under 16, but this makes up a very small fraction of all licenses

There is a sudden step down in all license types at 70, after which licenses must be renewed every 3 years (rather than the 10 years for younger drivers).

Source: sheet DRL0101-June2017.

Interactivity: mouse-over a data-point to see the corresponding value, or mouse-over an entry in the legend to highlight the corresponding curve.

How many men and women hold each class of licence?

Some classes of license are far more common than others. The most common are held by similar numbers of men and women, but there are a number of less common licence types that are held almost exclusively by men (such as Class H - Tracked vehicles). Additionally, for some classes (such as A and H) there are far more provisional than full licenses.

For context, see this official explanation of driving license categories.

Source: sheet DRL0110-June2017.

Interactivity: mouse-over a data-point to see the corresponding value.

How many points do drivers have?

The most common (non-zero) number of points is 3, followed by 6 and 9. Some people have received far more than this: one person has 51 points.

Source: sheet DRL0132-June2017.

Interactivity: mouse-over a data-point to see the corresponding value, or scale the y-axis to zoom-in on less common numbers of points.

How does number of points vary with age?

Young drivers have fewer points on average: they have had less opportunity to acquire them, and are perhaps initially more cautious because receiving 6 points within 2 years of passing the test would trigger automatic revocation of their license.

Source: sheet DRL0131 - June2017.

Interactivity: mouse-over a cell in the heat-map to see the corresponding value, and plots for people of the corresponding age or with the corresponding number of points. Click on the heat-map to lock these plots to a particular value, so that you cna mouse over their data points to see corresponding values.

These plots show the proportion of drivers of a particular age who have a particular number of points (scaled to be per thousand drivers of that age).

For context, see this official explanation of the penalty point system.

How does the proportion of provisional licenses differ between districts?

In a few postcode districts, approximately half of all driving licenses are provisional, but in others this figure is closer to 5%.

These districts occupy the lower-right corner of this plot: those in which the proportion of licenses that are provisional is above a threshold are highlighted in yellow. Dragging the circle on the line adjusts this threshold.

Source: sheet DRL0102-June2017.

Interactivity: mouse-over plotted points to see corresponding data values. Drag the black circle on the end ot the line to adjust the threshold proportion of provisional licenses - selected districts will be highlighted on the scatter-plot and map.

How does the proportion of people with points on their license differ between districts?

Some postcode districts contain only a single licensed driver, and 100% of their drivers have points. The average figure is 6%.

Source: sheet DRL0132-June2017 (normalized using driver counts from DRL0102-June2017).

Interactivity: mouse-over plotted districts to see corresponding data values.
